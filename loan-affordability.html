❓ How Loan Affordability Works

It calculates the maximum loan amount you can afford based on your monthly income, any existing EMIs, and the debt‑to‑income (DTI) ratio you set. It shows both the available monthly EMI capacity and the corresponding loan amount.

Enter your monthly take‑home income, any EMIs you already pay, and the DTI ratio (typically 30‑40%). Click Calculate to see how much more loan you can afford.

DTI is the percentage of your monthly income that goes toward debt payments (including the new EMI). Lenders use it to decide whether you can manage additional debt. A lower DTI means you can afford a larger loan.

If your existing EMIs already exceed the DTI limit you entered, there is no room for a new loan. You can adjust the DTI or reduce existing EMIs to see different scenarios.

Available EMI = (Monthly Income × DTI Ratio) − Existing EMI
Max Loan = Available EMI × (1 − (1 + r)−n) / r
where r = monthly interest rate (assumed 8% p.a.), n = loan tenure (assumed 20 years).
