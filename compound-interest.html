❓ How Compound Interest Works

Compound interest means you earn interest not only on your original investment (principal), but also on the interest that has already been added. Your money grows faster because you're earning “interest on interest.”

Enter your initial investment (Principal), the annual interest rate, the number of years you plan to stay invested, and how often the interest is compounded (monthly, quarterly, etc.). The calculator will show your final amount, total interest earned, and overall return percentage.

It’s how often the earned interest is added to your balance. For example, monthly compounding adds interest 12 times a year; annually adds it once. More frequent compounding results in slightly higher returns.

A = P × (1 + r/n)(n×t)
Where P = principal, r = annual interest rate (in decimal), n = number of compounding periods per year, t = time in years.
