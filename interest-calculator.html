❓ How Interest Calculation Works

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal amount. Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any interest that has already been added – so you earn "interest on interest." Compound interest grows your money faster.

Enter the principal (initial amount), the annual interest rate, and the time period in years. Then choose whether you want simple or compound interest. Click Calculate to see the interest earned and the final total amount.

Simple interest is often used for short‑term loans, car loans, and some fixed deposits where interest isn't reinvested. It's straightforward: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time.

Compound interest is common for savings accounts, investment portfolios, and long‑term deposits. It grows your money exponentially because interest is added to the balance at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, etc.).

Simple Interest: A = P(1 + rt)
Compound Interest: A = P(1 + r/n)nt
Where P = principal, r = annual rate (decimal), t = years, n = compounding frequency (monthly = 12).
