❓ How Investment Growth Works

It projects how a one‑time investment grows over a number of years, assuming a fixed annual return rate. You’ll see the final amount, total gain, and overall percentage return.

Enter your initial investment amount, the expected annual return (e.g., 10% for stock market averages), and the number of years you plan to stay invested. Click Calculate to see the projected result.

No. The calculator assumes a constant, steady return each year. Real investments fluctuate, and taxes may apply. Use this as a simple projection, not a guaranteed outcome.

This calculator models a single lump‑sum investment. If you want to simulate monthly or yearly contributions, try our Savings Goal Tracker or Compound Interest calculator with recurring deposits.

Final Amount = Initial Investment × (1 + Annual Return/100)Years
Gain = Final Amount – Initial Investment
Total Return % = (Gain / Initial Investment) × 100
