❓ How Freelancer Income Works

It estimates your net monthly income as a freelancer. You enter your hourly rate, total hours worked per month, taxes (like GST), and any business expenses. The calculator shows your gross income, tax amount, income after taxes, and final net income after expenses.

Fill in your hourly rate (what you charge clients), the number of hours you work in a month, the tax percentage (e.g., 18% for GST), and your monthly business expenses (internet, software, etc.). Then click Calculate.

These are costs you incur to run your freelance business, such as internet bills, software subscriptions, equipment, marketing, co‑working space, etc. They are deducted after taxes.

The calculator applies a flat percentage you enter to the gross income. Real‑world taxes may have slabs, deductions, or different rules. Use it as a quick estimate and consult a tax professional for exact filings.

Gross = Hourly Rate × Hours
Tax = Gross × (Tax % / 100)
Net = Gross − Tax − Expenses
