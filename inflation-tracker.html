❓ How Inflation Tracking Works

It tells you the future value of your money after accounting for inflation. You enter a current amount, an annual inflation rate, and a time period. The calculator shows the adjusted (decreased) purchasing power and how much value was lost.

Enter the amount of money you have today, the expected inflation rate (e.g., 5.5% per year), and the number of years you want to look ahead. Click Calculate to see how much that money will be worth in today’s terms.

It’s the equivalent purchasing power of your current money after the inflation period. For example, ₹1,00,000 today might only buy what ₹58,500 would buy after 10 years at 5.5% inflation.

The calculator assumes a constant annual inflation rate for simplicity. Real inflation varies year by year. Use an average rate for the best estimate.

Adjusted Value = Amount / (1 + Inflation Rate)Years
Loss = Amount − Adjusted Value
Loss % = (Loss / Amount) × 100
