❓ How Retirement Planning Works

It estimates the total retirement corpus you’ll accumulate by the time you retire, based on your current savings, monthly contributions, expected return, and time horizon. It also shows how long that corpus might last and a safe annual withdrawal amount (5%).

Enter your current age, desired retirement age, existing savings, how much you contribute every month, and the expected annual return on your investments. Also provide your life expectancy to see the retirement duration.

The calculator uses a 5% annual withdrawal rate, which is a common rule‑of‑thumb for sustainable retirement income. It means you could withdraw 5% of your total corpus each year without exhausting the fund too quickly.

This calculator focuses on nominal returns and the final corpus. For a clearer picture of purchasing power, you can separately use the Inflation Tracker to see how inflation might affect your future withdrawals.

The future value of a lump sum plus a series of monthly contributions is computed using the compound interest formula with monthly compounding. The retirement duration is simply the difference between life expectancy and retirement age.
