❓ How Overtime Calculation Works

It calculates how much extra pay you’ll receive for working overtime hours. You enter your regular hourly rate, the number of overtime hours, and the overtime multiplier (e.g., 1.5 for time‑and‑a‑half).

Fill in your normal hourly wage, the total overtime hours you’ve worked, and the overtime rate multiplier (commonly 1.5 or 2). The calculator shows the base overtime amount and the final overtime earnings.

It’s the factor by which your regular hourly rate is multiplied for overtime hours. For example, 1.5 means time‑and‑a‑half pay, 2 means double pay. The multiplier may be set by law or company policy.
