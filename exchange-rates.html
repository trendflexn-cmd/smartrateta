❓ How Exchange Rates Work

The table shows how much 1 unit of each foreign currency is worth in US Dollars (USD). For example, if EUR shows 1.087, it means 1 Euro = 1.087 USD.

The rates displayed are indicative mid‑market rates, updated regularly. They are accurate enough for budgeting and quick reference. Use the Currency Converter for live conversions.

All rates are shown relative to the US Dollar (USD). So each number tells you how many US Dollars equal one unit of the foreign currency. For example, GBP 1.265 = £1 buys $1.265.

These are mid‑market rates – the true midpoint between buy and sell. Banks and transfer services add a margin (spread) and fees, so the final amount you pay or receive will be slightly different. For exact costs, use our Transfer Fee Calculator.

This page uses manually updated rates for demonstration. In a production environment, we fetch live rates via API every few seconds. You can check the footer for the last update date.
